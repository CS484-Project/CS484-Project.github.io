How to Use the Map


General Use

  1. The target of our research was Alaska, so let's click and drag on the map up and to the left a bit until we find Alaska. It should be a large open area to the left of Canada.

  2. Once you've found Alaska, we can navigate over the 'Land Cover Dynamics' tab on the left hand side of the map.

  3. Now we can select 'Land Cover Dynamics' and click on the 'Tree Cover Loss' button so it slides to the right.

  4. At this point a bunch of red dots should appear on the map! This represents large clusters of tree area coverage when measured within the given year on the timeline. However! It is important to note that Tree Cover Loss does not equal deforestation - but, it is a large contributing factor.

  5. Now, using the slider that appeared, you can manipulate from what years of the dataset are showing. This way you can see the progression of tree-cover loss from 2001 - 2022.


Advanced Features

In the modal we were just navigating in, there are three tabs at the top. If you navigate to the first one from the right, and when you hover it says 'Analysis', clicking on this tab will give you instructions on all of the tools available at your disposal. Once you get a hang of these tools, you can click on the middle tab on the right-hand side menu. Here you can dive further into some data analysis of the shapes you made.
